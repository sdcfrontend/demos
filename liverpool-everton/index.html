Key Moments

1992

Ian Rush breaks Liverpool goal record with 287th in 2-2 draw with Man United

1994

The Kop demolished after 1-0 defeat to Norwich and makes way for all-seater grandstand

Robbie Fowler scores hat-trick in four minutes and 35 seconds against Arsenal

1995

Steve McManaman shines as Liverpool beat Bolton to lift League Cup

1996

Bob Paisley passes away

1997

Michael Owen becomes youngest Liverpool goalscorer aged 17 years and 144 days

2001

Beat Birmingham on penalties to lift League Cup

Michael Owen inspires Liverpool to FA Cup triumph over Arsenal in Cardiff

Lift first European silverware in 17 years with 5-4 win over Alaves

2002

Secure runners-up spot in Premier League– their joint-highest finish to date

2003

Win the League Cup again after beating Man United 2-0 at the Millennium Stadium

2004

Steven Gerrard sends Liverpool into CL knock-out phase with third goal against Olympiacos

2005

Miracle of Istanbul: Liverpool lift CL trophy after beating AC Milan

2006

Beat West Ham in penalty shoot-out to lift FA Cup

2007

Americans George Gillett and Tom Hicks buy Liverpool from David Moores

Lose CL final to AC Milan in Athens

Record biggest win in CL history with 8-0 scoreline against Besiktas at Anfield

2010

Fenway Sports Group buys Liverpool

2012

Win first cup for six years as the Reds beat Cardiff City after a penalty shoot-out at the new Wembley

Campaigners for Justice see original inquest verdicts overturned

2014

Narrowly miss out on Premier League title

2016

Lose to Sevilla in Europa League final

New 20,500-seater Main Stand opened to fans

Ben Woodburn breaks Michael Owen’s record to become Liverpool’s youngest scorer aged 17 years and 45 days

2017

Club celebrates 125th birthday

Ronnie Moran passes away

2018

Mohamed Salah wins Premier League Golden Boot with 32 goals in first season at Anfield

Liverpool lose 3-1 to Real Madrid in CL final

1994

Great Escape: Overturn two-goal deficit to beat Wimbledon 3-2 on final day of season to secure top-flight survival

Park End Stand opens with 2-2 draw against Aston Villa

Joe Royle oversees 2-0 win against Liverpool in his first game at the helm

1995

Daniel Amokachi comes on as a substitute and scores twice to secure 4-1 win over Spurs in FA Cup semi-final

Win fifth FA Cup with 1-0 win against Man United at Wembley

1998

Great Escape Pt II

Peter Johnson steps down as chairman and Sir Philip Carter takes over

1999

Merseyside derby sparks a brawl at Goodison Park with three players seeing red

Peter Johnson sells club to consortium led by Bill Kenwright

2002

Wayne Rooney makes Premier League debut at 16 years old during 2-2 draw with Tottenham

2004

Bill Kenwright replaces Sir Philip Carter as chairman

2005

James Vaughan becomes youngest Everton goalscorer at the age of 16 years and 271 days

Finish fourth in the Premier League - ahead of Liverpool

2006

Brian Labone passes away

2007

Alan Ball passes away

Move to Finch Farm training ground

2008

Achieve their most successful European run in 23 years, reaching last 16 of UEFA Cup

2009

Beat Man United in penalty shoot-out at new Wembley in FA Cup semi-final

2011

Bulldoze Bellefield training ground site

2013

David Moyes confirms he will leave club

Fans vote for new club crest

Finish season with record points haul under new boss Roberto Martinez

2015

Howard Kendall passes away

Sir Philip Carter passes away

Romelu Lukaku scores in eight successive league game during 3-2 defeat to Leicester

2016

Tim Howard becomes first player to make 350 Premier League appearances for Everton

Farhad Moshiri becomes new major shareholder

2017

Alex Young passes away

Lukaku scores becomes Everton’s top Premier League scorer with 60th during X-X over Spurs

Wayne Rooney returns to club for 2017/18 season before moving to MLS side DC United

2018

Marcel Brands appointed new Director of Football

Ray Wilson passes away
