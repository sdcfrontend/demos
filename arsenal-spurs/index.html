Key Moments

1993

CUP DOUBLE! Beat Tottenham en-route to winning FA Cup and also lift League Cup.

1994

Claim first European trophy with 1-0 win over Parma in Cup Winners’ Cup.

1995

George Graham sacked

1996

Arsene Wenger appointed manager.

1997

Ian Wright breaks Cliff Basin’s record to become club’s all-time top scorer with 179 goals.

Dennis Bergkamp scores hat-trick during 3-3 draw at Leicester – capped with that iconic goal of the season.

1998

ANOTHER DOUBLE! Win first ever Premier League title and lift FA Cup.

2001

David Rocastle passes away.

Reach Champions League quarter-finals but lose 2-2 to Valencia on away goals on aggregate.

Sign Spurs centre-back Sol Campbell

2002

ANOTHER! Win Premier League and FA Cup, with Sylian Wiltord scoring during 1-0 win over Manchester United to clinch double.

Youth academy releases Harry Kane at nine years old.

2003

Robert Pires scores winning goal as Arsenal beat Southampton 1-0 in the FA Cup final.

2004

Chelsea eliminate Arsenal in Champions League quarter-finals after 2-1 second-leg defeat at Highbury

INVINCIBLES: Win third Premier League title without losing a single game.

2005

Beat Manchester United on penalties to lift FA Cup at Millennium Stadium.

Thierry Henry breaks Ian Wright’s record to become club’s all-time top scorer after netting two goals against Sparta Prague in Champions League.

2006

Become English first team to beat Real Madrid at the Bernabeu but lose 2-1 to Barcelona in Champions league final.

Henry scores hat-trick during 4-2 win over Wigan in last ever game at Highbury.

Move to Emirates Stadium after 93 years at Highbury.

2008

Draw 1-1 with Liverpool at Emirates in Champions League quarter-final first leg, but lose 4-2 at Anfield.

2009

Breeze past Villarreal in Champions League quarter-finals but lose 4-1 on aggregate to Manchester United in semis.

2010

Hold Barcelona to 2-2 draw at Emirates in Champions League quarter-final first leg but lose 4-1 at Nou Camp.

2014

Beat Hull City 3-2 in extra-time to lift FA Cup

2015

Cruise to 4-0 win over Aston Villa in FA Cup final.

Don Howe passes away.

2017

Lift FA Cup after 2-1 win over newly-crowned Premier League champions Chelsea.

2018

Arsene Wenger announces he will step down after 22 years in charge.

Hold Atletico Madrid to 1-1 draw in Europa League semi-final first leg at Emirates but lose 1-0 in Madrid.
