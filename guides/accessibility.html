Accessibility Guidelines

Why is accessibility important?

At Sky it is important that our products and services are accessible to everyone who wants to use them, regardless of their abilities. Accessibility is not just our social and legal responsibility, it is an essential part of how we serve our customers.

What we are committed to

Our websites must meet at least level AA of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG 2.1) and work on the most commonly used assistive technologies such as screen readers and speech recognition tools.

We will also include people with disabilities in our design and user testing process and provide an accessibility statement that explains how accessible our products are.

To ensure our products are accessible to all we must consider the needs of a diverse range of users.

Visual
Designing for users with sight impairments
  • Use text with high colour contrast and a readable font size
  • Use well formed HTML with headers and ensure images and videos have text descriptions
  • Use text labels and shapes to convey meaning in graphics rather than only colour
  • Use a logical, linear layout and ensure interactive elements such as buttons are in context with their content
Hearing
Designing for users with hearing impairments
  • Write in plain English and avoid complicated words and jargon
  • Use subtitles or provide text alternatives for videos
  • Use a logical, linear layout and avoid complex menus and interactions
  • Break up large blocks of text with headers, images and videos
Cognitive
Designing for users with autism and dyslexia
  • Avoid large blocks of text - use headers, bullet points, images, and videos to make content easier to digest
  • Avoid bright high contrast colors and overly complex and cluttered layouts
  • Write in plain English and avoid figures of speech, idioms and jargon
  • Content in video, audio, images and graphics are easier for some users to understand than large blocks of text
Physical
Designing for users with physical disabilies
  • Use large hit areas for interactive elements such as buttons and avoid grouping multiple interactions too closely together
  • Ensure all content is accessible using keyboard and assistive technologies
  • Try to use shortcuts to navigate large amounts of content, and avoid the requirement for lots of scrolling, interactions or typing
  • Make sure forms and interactive content does not have a short time out period so users are able to take a break

WCAG Guidelines

It is a government requirement for our digital ecosystem to be AA compliant. For more information on how to be WCAG AA compliant visit the WCAG website
