State of the Nations

Introduction

The vast majority of Britons believe the UK public is deeply divided amid the political turmoil over Brexit and fear for the future of the UK economy, a Sky Data poll has found.

The wide-ranging survey has shed light on the mood of the nation as the UK prepares to leave the European Union, revealing the level of distrust felt towards institutions - from the government to the mainstream media.

In one of the most troubling findings, a significant proportion of the country believe the UK public is racist and Islamophobic.

But surprisingly, given the uncertainty facing the country, most Britons say they are happy in their lives and have a more positive outlook about their personal futures than the UK’s as a whole. Here, Sky News delves into the results to reveal the state of the nation.

Divided UK

Three quarters (75%) of Britons believe the UK is divided, with the country’s younger generation most affected, according to the poll.

Some 72% of people aged 18 to 24 described the British public as divided, compared to just 64% of those aged 75 or older.

But more than third of Britons (34%) admitted they had little understanding of events in British politics.

Just one in five (21%) people said they believed they could make a difference in how the country is governed.

Personal happiness

Despite the political turmoil over Brexit, the Sky Data poll revealed the vast majority of Britons are happy with their lives.

Seven in 10 (70%) said they were happy in life in general, compared to 17% who said they were unhappy.

Over 75s were the happiest age group (77%), while 65% of 18 to 24-year-olds said they were happy.

In regions around Britain, Wales reported the highest level of happiness (77%), compared to 67% in the Midlands.

British people were most happy about their family and friends (86%) followed by their health (67%) and finances (64%).

Some 93% of Britons said their finances mattered to their happiness, compared to 94% who said their health.

Just 67% said their physical appearance mattered to how happy they were, while just 24% of people said social media was important to their happiness.
