Introduction

Here Sky News breaks down the numbers and some of the stories behind them.

Dennis Moss How the Aberfan disaster led to a 40-year NHS career

Dennis Moss spent 41 years as a paramedic in Cardiff and the surrounding valleys after he helped with the recovery efforts from the Aberfan disaster.

As one of the youngest responders on the scene, Mr Moss had the harrowing task of searching for and removing bodies from the rubble, but the disaster sparked a fire in him to help people, and set him on his career path.

Abishek Rolands Junior Doctor

×

Abishek Rolands What it takes to become an NHS doctor

Abishek Rolands is one of approximately 55,000 junior doctors working in England, having graduated from Imperial College London.

The path to becoming a junior doctor is a tough one, with huge demands placed upon every student before they even enter the profession.

Courses can last as long as six years - double the amount of time most people spend at university - and it only gets more difficult from there.

Here, Dr Rolands tells Sky News what it takes to make it. Read more

Navin Leanage Junior Doctor

Navin Leanage Why I'm so proud to work for the NHS

Navin Leanage has only been a junior doctor since 2016, but the pressure placed on the health service in recent years has already made an impression.

Staffing is an issue, funding is tight and stress is high, and yet for Dr Leanage, the NHS remains a shining example of what makes the UK so special.

Here, he writes for Sky News about how proud he is to be part of the incredible work being done by staff to keep the NHS operating at such a high standard.

Tarun Sundersingh How Great Ormond Street got the best out of me

Tarun Sundersingh was born with a rare condition that affected his ability to walk and move.

He was treated at Great Ormond Street Hospital (GOSH) throughout his childhood and adolescence and now - at the age of 22 - works in the technology department at its children's charity

Aneira Thomas
×

Aneira Thomas Meet the first person to be born on the NHS

In July 1948, Aneira Thomas became the first baby to be born in the new National Health Service.

Named by the doctor who tended to her mother after the founder of the health service, health secretary Aneurin Bevan, she would then go on to become a nurse.

Here, she writes for Sky News about sharing a 70th birthday with the NHS and how much it has changed during their lifetimes.

Read more

Tony Acres How the NHS has given me a lifetime of benefit

Tony Acres is incredibly grateful for what he describes as a lifetime of benefit from the NHS.

He credits a procedure on his hand with allowing him to continue his career, paramedics with saving his son after a horrific motorbike crash, and a vital operation keeping his daughter from years bound to a wheelchair. Writing for Sky News, he speaks of his eternal gratitude for the work of the health service.

Common conditions

Cancer
×

Cancer

  • 360,000 new cases in the UK every year
  • 53% of new cases are breast, prostate, lung and bowel cancers
  • 36% of cases are people over-75
  • 50% of people diagnosed survive for 10+ years
  • 50% of people born after 1960 will be diagnosed with cancer
  • 40% of cases are preventable

Cancer Research UK

Heart Disease
×

Heart Disease

  • 420 people die of cardiovascular disease in the UK every day
  • 180 people die of a heart attack in the UK every day
  • 545 people go to hospital due to a heart attack in the UK every day
  • 12 babies are diagnosed with a heart defect in the UK every day
  • Healthcare costs related to CVD an est. £9bn per year
  • Highest premature CVD death rates – Manchester (141 per 100,000) and Glasgow (139 per 100,000)

British Heart Foundation, 2018

Obesity
×

Obesity

  • 26% of UK adults are obese
  • More than a third of UK adults are overweight
  • 28% of UK children are overweight or obese
  • 40% of UK adults do not complete recommended levels of physical activity
  • 75% of UK adults do not have five portions of fruit and veg a day
  • 25% of UK adults exceed guidelines for weekly alcohol intake

British Heart Foundation, 2018

Dementia
×

Dementia

  • 850,000 people living with dementia in the UK
  • 33% of people born in the UK this year will develop dementia in their lifetime
  • 7% of people over-65 in the UK have dementia
  • 2 million people will have dementia in the UK in 2050
  • Dementia is most common in people aged 80 and up

Alzheimer’s Research UK, 2018

Diabetes

  • 3.7m new diabetes cases in 2016/17
  • 3.1m in England
  • 289,000 in Scotland
  • 192,000 in Wales
  • 92,500 in Northern Ireland
  • 5 million people will have been diagnosed by 2025
  • 630,000 people have diabetes but have not been diagnosed
  • 90% of people with diabetes have Type 2

Diabetes UK, 2017
