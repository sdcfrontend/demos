City UNITED Evolution of a rivalry

By Adam Smith @DataSmith101 and Arron Sekhri

It was forged in Victorian times and spanned 137 years to present day - but the Manchester rivalry has never been stronger.

Sir Alex Ferguson claimed his first top-flight crown during the inaugural Premier League season and proceeded to claim 12 more over his following 21 years at the helm.

In contrast, neighbours City had a tumultuous period during the early Premier League years, slipping to the third tier and back - only cementing top-flight status in the noughties.

But how have the teams evolved over the past 26 years, which players and managers have come and gone, which kits have they donned and which moments have been inscribed in club folklore?

United dominate early years

Man Utd win seven out of first nine Premier League titles

United are the most successful club in England during the Premier League era, lifting the trophy in 13 out of 26 seasons.

But that dominance has been on the wane since Sergio Aguero scored a last-gasp title winner against QPR in 2011, with City claiming three titles to United's one.

It's now been five years since United last claimed a Premier League crown, the same year Fergie stepped down after 25 years in charge.

However, under Jose Mourinho, United recorded their highest finish during the post-Ferguson era last term, as runners-up behind City - albeit 19 points adrift.

Fergie time

Owen scores late winner in 2009

The world gasped when United splashed £93.25m on Juventus midfielder Paul Pogba - but one year later that world-record fee was more than doubled when Paris Saint-Germain signed Neymar.

Despite City now being synonymous with free-spending, the club have still only hit the £60m threshold with record signing Riyad Mahrez this summer.

However, the Cityzens have actually spent more on transfers than United during the entire Premier League era - despite spending five seasons in England's lower leagues,

City's biggest outlays came during Pep Guardiola's overhaul during his first two seasons in charge, while United splashed the most cash during Louis van Gaal's first term.

Rooney's magic moment

Spectacular winner sends Old Trafford into raptures in 2011

Few players endure every fixture of a season in the modern game, but, back in the day, it was far more common.

United's formidable defensive shield of Steve Bruce, Gary Pallister and 'keeper Peter Schmeichel all played every game of the 1992/93 season, as did City's David White.

That feat has only been achieved on 14 occasions since, of which 12 endured a less gruelling 38-game season - when the league was reduced from 22 clubs to 20 at the start of 1995/96.

And while David de Gea's plaudits allude to his three successive Player of the Season awards at Old Trafford, City's Richard Dunne managed four on the trot between 2005 and 2008.

Derby demolition

Balotelli celebrates as City thrash United 6-1 in 2011

Fergie's 25 years as manager is trumped only by a few throughout history - but the Red Devils turned over three permanent bosses in as many years after the Scot stepped down.

Including caretaker managers, City have appointed 17 managers during the modern era and have an average term of under two years - with Kevin Keegan, Roberto Mancini and Joe Royle lasting longest.

In terms of head to heads, United earn bragging rights 21-13 - with eight fixtures ending in stalemate.

But City's 55 goals are just eight shy of United's total, and the Reds have failed to win consecutive derbies since winning four in a row between 2008 and 2010.

More recently, City won four successive derbies under Roberto Mancini and Manuel Pellegrini before a 4-2 defeat in 2015.

The goal fests? United won a 4-3 thriller in 2009, but City exacted revenge two years later with a 6-1 romp at Old Trafford - which remains the greatest winning margin to date.
